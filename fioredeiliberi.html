Who is Fiore

Fiore de'i Liberi, was a late 14th century knight, diplomat, and sword master. He was born in Cividale del Friuli, a town in the Patriarchal State of Aquileia (in the Friuli region of modern-day Italy).

Fiore wrote that he had a natural inclination to the martial arts and began training at a young age, ultimately studying with “countless” masters from both Italic and Germanic lands. He had ample opportunity to interact with both, being born in the Holy Roman Empire and later traveling widely in the northern Italian states.

He was a successful master who taught numerous Knights and nobles, and based on his financial power, he left a luxurious book for the time: The Flower of Battle(Fiore di Battaglia), one of the most important books in the modern-day restoration of Historical western martial arts.

He is believed to have died since 1420 and is the oldest of the masters whose life and death dates can be estimated.

Unfortunately, not all of these encounters were friendly: Fiore wrote of meeting many “false” or unworthy masters in his travels. He further mentions that on five separate occasions he was forced to fight duels for his honor against certain of these masters who he described as envious because he refused to teach them his art; the duels were all fought with sharp swords, unarmored except for gambesons and chamois gloves, and he won each without injury.
